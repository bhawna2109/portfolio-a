Postman's find and replace

Designed and shipped the experience to perform bulk refactor in Postman.

View find and replace prototype

The need

We had been getting several feature requests over the years around supporting bulk refactoring in Postman. Some of our users were working with hundreds of collections and environments, and they wanted an easy way to replace things in bulk, rather than having to do it one by one.

My major learning from this project was realising and understanding the iterative nature of design. Sometimes it can take several iterations to reach a design which finally 'works'. Creating, sharing with team members, receiving feedback and iterating is the key. There's ALWAYS scope for improvement.

Responsibilities

As the only designer working on this feature, my responsibilities included:

  • Understanding the underlying need of the users by going through feature requests on github and other channels
  • Learning about bulk refactoring in code
  • Wireframing: Understanding the Postman interface and creating wireframes to identify the best real estate to introduce this feature
  • Creating high-fidelity mocks and prototypes for the final solution.
  • Working closely with engineers to ensure high-quality implementation
  • Writing documentation

Early iterations

These are a few early iterations for the feature - highlights the importance of 'trimming down' a design. Adding interface elements is easy, but taking them off is hard. Thus, it is very important to utilise the real estate judiciously and keep ONLY the absolutely required interface elements for a cleaner and minimalistic design that 'works'.
